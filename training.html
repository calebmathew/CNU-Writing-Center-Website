Writing Consultant Training

All Writing Consultants are required to successfully complete the writing-intensive course ENGL 339: Tutoring in the Writing Center. This class introduces students to the current best practices in Writing Center theory and practice, including how to develop a tutoring approach that is based on a Universal Design for Learning principle. In short, all consultants are trained to work with student-writers from all backgrounds and disciplines, with all skill levels.

Interested in working for us? ENGL 339 is taught every Fall and Spring semester. Please email the Writing Center Director, Dr. Nicole Emmelhainz, with questions or for more information.


Writing Consultant Research

As part of ENGL 339, all students propose, research, draft and revise a Writing Center research paper.

Additionally, Writing Center Consultants have participated in regional and international Writing Center conferences, with travel and lodging support from the Writing Center, the Office of Undergraduate Research and Creative Activity, and the Student Assembly. Most recently, consultants have participated in the Southeastern Writing Center Association’s annual conference and the International Writing Center’s Annual Conference.
